Employment

Vetting Officer

New Zealand Police National Headquarters Wellington, New Zealand
(March 2016-December 2016)

For this role I had to hit the ground running in an established team. I had to constantly demonstrate my analysing and decision making capabilities while growing key relationships with internal and external clients. I had to establish and maintain appropriate internal and external working relationships with Police Staff, government agencies and the general public. Some of the many tasks I was involved in are: Processing vetting applications for approved agencies such as New Zealand Immigration, Department of Internal Affairs, Ministry of Justice, NZTA etc while keeping to the strict service level agreement. Responding to phone calls and correspondence from individuals and approved organisations for guidance on vetting processes and the release of information. Evaluating all positive identifications, ensuring that names and aliases are matched and responding to Official Information Act and Privacy Act requests for release of personal information. Evaluating CRN(Criminal Record Number) transfers which are unique numbers assigned to a specific or singular charge to reconcile entries with the correct identities and arranging for Ministry of Justice to transfer charge details. Provided advice on National Intelligence Application (NIA) data entry to Police staff and updating personal information where required. Resolving identity issues in NIA.
